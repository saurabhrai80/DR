About Me

Hi, I'm Saurabh, and I am a researcher with a focus on materials science and computational modeling. Below are some of my projects and experiences.

Education

  • PhD in Materials Science - Shiv Nadar University (2021-2025)

    • Worked on dynamic stiffness methods for nanostructures. Developed FGM material models using Abaqus with UMAT subroutines and validated results. Explored analytical modeling of thermoelectric materials.

  • M.Tech in Mechanical Engineering - [IIT Ropar] (2017-2019)

    • Focused on Incremental Forming and Friction Stir Processing. Performed simulations and experiments on friction stir processing (FEA) for predicting optimal feed and spin for spindles.

  • Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering - Ajak Kumar Garg (2012-2016)

Projects

Incremental Forming Simulation and Experimentation

Period: 2017-2019

During my M.Tech, I worked on experimental and simulation studies of Incremental Forming under the guidance of Prof. Anupam Aggrawal. I focused on simulating the forming process and validating it with experimental data.

Friction Stir Processing (FEA)

Period: 2017-2019

Worked on friction stir processing to predict the optimal feed and spin for spindles. Used FEA to model and optimize the process for better material properties and productivity.

Formability Analysis of Beta Titanium

Period: 2020-2021

During my tenure at BITS Hyderabad, I worked on a DRDO-sponsored project on formability analysis of beta titanium under the guidance of Dr. Kurra Suresh. The project involved advanced material testing and simulation.

FGM Material Modeling in Abaqus

Period: 2021-2025

As part of my PhD, I developed a Finite Gradient Material (FGM) model in Abaqus using UMAT subroutine and validated the results with experimental data. The work focused on materials with graded properties.

Thermoelectric Materials Efficiency Modeling

Period: 2021-2025

Worked on analytical modeling to study the efficiency of thermoelectric materials for energy applications. The research aimed to optimize material properties for better thermoelectric performance.
